Yeah, We Get It. We Can Never Learn Math.

People like to say math is hard. Math is just not for everyone. Some people just can’t learn it. Those people are dumb.

Among the people who math is not for are the artists. Let me start by clarifying that this is not actually true. It is in fact abundantly false because artists are equally equipped to learn math, if not more. Math is an incredibly visual language. Teachers who are not visual thinkers themselves tend to ignore this. Whether they do not consider teaching visually or assume it is too complex for their students, visual learners are so rarely catered to in most teachers' curriculums. As an artist myself, this breaks my heart. I have worked as a mathematics tutor for students of kindergarten math to Calculus II, and seeing how many visual learners have been turned away from math altogether is simply unacceptable. I will describe using both my experiences as well as the writings of _____ to show how an artist can learn math, how it can be taught, and how it can be extrapolated if not presented in an inherantly visual representation.

How an Artist Learns Math: Breaking It Down and Learning to Chill

The most useful advice I’ve given as a math tutor is to break it down. Come back with me to the Spring of 2016. I was barely seventeen years old in my second semester of college at a very rigorous Liberal Arts School. My dad was getting a divorce with his third wife, and it was messy. I had a cruel boyfriend who I was too naive to let go of, and I was spiralling out of control. Meanwhile, I was taking a Calculus heavy Physics II course (without a strong understanding of Calculus) at a school where nothing was easy, and everything was made to make you think. I couldn’t think, and I felt like a failure, and when I looked at those pages of equations, that is what they were. They were pages of equations, and I felt like maybe I’m not cut out to learn such a high level of math. You might be like, “Well, duh, Abby.. Pages of equations, that’s what math is!” but you would be wrong! The secret to learning math is seeing it as an algorithm. This is a threatening word that really just means a process. To show you what I mean, here is a generic example:
